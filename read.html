N60 Billion CBN Scheme: How Young Nigerian Entrepreneurs Can Access Funding for Their Agribusiness

PUBLISHED THU, APR 2 2024 2:52 PM

The Nigerian government, through the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), is offering a N60 billion funding opportunity specifically targeted at young, aspiring agricultural entrepreneurs. This initiative, known as the Agric Small and Medium Enterprises Investment Scheme (AGSMEIS), aims to boost the growth of agribusinesses and empower young people to contribute meaningfully to the nation's economic development.

Unlocking Opportunities for Young Agripreneurs in Nigeria The AGSMEIS program recognizes the crucial role small and medium enterprises (SMEs) play in driving economic progress. By specifically targeting the agricultural sector, the scheme seeks to:

  • Enhance access to finance: SMEs often struggle to secure funding, hindering their growth potential. AGSMEIS aims to bridge this gap by providing financial resources to young agribusiness owners.
  • Create employment opportunities: As these businesses flourish, they have the potential to generate much-needed jobs within the Nigerian economy.
  • Develop the agricultural value chain: AGSMEIS encourages sustainable agricultural practices and the development of a robust agricultural value chain.
  • Strengthen the capacity of young agripreneurs: The program fosters the managerial capabilities of young business owners, nurturing them into potential leaders in the agricultural sector.

    • Who Can Benefit from the AGSMEIS Scheme?

    The AGSMEIS initiative is designed to support young, eligible Nigerians with established or aspiring agribusiness ventures. Here's a breakdown of the eligibility criteria:

  • Compliant Business Practices: Your business must adhere to the regulations outlined in the Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA), including filing annual returns and maintaining audited financial statements.
  • Tax Compliance: Upholding all applicable tax laws and submitting regular tax returns is mandatory.
  • Application through Participating Banks: You must apply for the loan through a bank that participates in the AGSMEIS program.

    • Responsibilities of Beneficiaries

    Once approved for funding under the AGSMEIS scheme, beneficiaries are expected to:

  • Utilize funds prudently: The loan should be used solely for the intended business purposes outlined in your application.
  • Maintain meticulous records: Keep comprehensive and up-to-date records of all business activities related to the AGSMEIS funding
  • Transparency and Compliance: Be prepared to present your company's financial records and structures for inspection by authorized bodies when necessary.
  • Adherence to Guidelines: Operate your business within the established guidelines of the AGSMEIS program.
  • Regular Reporting: Submit monthly financial and operational reports to the Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) by the stipulated deadline.

    • Requirements and Application Process for CBN AGSMEIS Loans

    Securing an AGSMEIS loan involves specific steps and requirements:

    Mandatory Training: Before applying, you must complete a one-week training course offered by an Entrepreneurship Development Institute (EDI) certified by NIRSAL MFB. Upon successful completion, you will receive a certificate.

    Online Loan Application: Following the training, visit the NIRSAL MFB website and apply for the AGSMEIS loan through their online portal. Remember to upload your EDI completion certificate.

    Application Process:

  • Access the NIRSAL MFB website (nmfb.com.ng).
  • Click on the "AGSMEIS loan" section.
  • Select the Ministry of Agriculture as your chosen EDI.
  • Submit your online application.
  • NIRSAL MFB will forward your application to the CBN for review.
  • You may be contacted for a business interview to assess your venture.
  • Upon successful evaluation, the loan will be granted, and the funds will be transferred to your NMFB account.
  • Unsuccessful candidates will receive feedback on their applications

    • Additional Points to Note:

  • The loan amount will be deposited directly into your NMFB account.
  • NIRSAL MFB's certified vendors will handle the procurement of any necessary equipment or machinery for your business.
  • Working capital will be accessible for withdrawal from your account only after fulfilling all terms and conditions set forth by the scheme.
